# Yemen-Houthis-Red Sea Triangle Deepening

## Summary

The Houthis-Yemen relationship exhibits anomalous weakness (2.5) compared to Houthis-Israel (4.5) despite Yemen serving as the Houthis' operational base. This investigation reveals that the Houthis function as a parallel non-state governance structure rather than an extension of Yemeni state sovereignty, deliberately maintaining strategic ambiguity that serves Iranian proxy interests while complicating international attribution and response.

## Key Findings

- **Sovereignty Fracture:** Yemen exists as a "memory of unity" rather than a functioning state since 2014-2015; the Houthis govern Sana'a and northern territories through parallel institutions (courts, taxation, military) without claiming formal state sovereignty
- **Deliberate Non-State Posture:** Unlike Hezbollah's integration into Lebanese institutions, the Houthis maintain institutional separation from Yemeni state structures, preserving operational flexibility and Iranian plausible deniability
- **Identity Weaponization:** The Houthis-Israel connection burns hotter because "Yemen" offers contested ideological ground (PLC, STC, Saleh legacy all contest that identity), while "Israel" provides unilateral crystalline purpose—no Yemeni consensus required for the 2022 "state of war" declaration
- **Proxy Chain Architecture:** Iran → Houthis → Yemen represents support flowing *through* a movement *toward* disruption of recognized order, not its reinforcement
- **Fragmentation as Enabler:** Yemen's shattered sovereignty directly enables Houthi unilateralism in Red Sea operations without parliamentary debate or presidential authorization

## Entities

- **Supreme Political Council (SPC)** (governance body) — Houthi parallel state structure established 2016, exercises de facto authority in northern Yemen without international recognition
- **Presidential Leadership Council (PLC)** (governance body) — Internationally recognized Yemeni authority based in Aden, controls limited territory and competes with Houthi governance
- **Southern Transitional Council (STC)** (secessionist movement) — UAE-backed de facto autonomous power in southern Yemen, further fragments Yemeni sovereignty
- **Ansar Allah** (armed movement) — Formal organizational identity of the Houthi movement, emphasizing religious/political movement over state institution
- **"State of war" declaration (2022)** (strategic act) — Unilateral Houthi claim establishing belligerent status with Israel, bypassing all Yemeni state procedures
- **Ali Abdullah Saleh's republic** (historical regime) — Contested legacy representing alternative Yemeni national narrative that Houthis reject

## Connections

- Iran → arms/funding → Ansar Allah
- Ansar Allah → parallel governance → Supreme Political Council
- Supreme Political Council || Presidential Leadership Council — contested sovereignty
- Presidential Leadership Council || Southern Transitional Council — territorial fragmentation
- Ansar Allah → unilateral declaration → "State of war" declaration (2022)
- "State of war" declaration (2022) → targets → Israel
- Yemen's fragmentation → enables → Houthi operational freedom
- Non-state status → creates → diplomatic attribution complexity
- Iran → Houthis → Yemen — proxy chain (support flows through, not to)
