# Yemen State Fragmentation and Actor Mapping

## Summary

Yemen's conflict has evolved from binary Houthi-IRG confrontation into a **quadrilateral sovereignty contest** with competing territorial, economic, and infrastructural claims. This research elevates Yemen from an underexplored node (10 mentions, 1 connection) to a fully contextualized conflict zone, mapping 15+ distinct actors across military, political, and humanitarian domains. The analysis reveals critical misalignments between nominal alliances—particularly Saudi-UAE competition within the anti-Houthi coalition—and identifies infrastructure control (energy grids, telecommunications, financial systems) as the decisive terrain of state fragmentation. The findings directly challenge simplified "Houthi = Yemen" framing that weakens predictive models of Red Sea security and regional conflict diffusion.

## Key Findings

### Territorial Control: From Tripartite to Atomized Fragmentation

- **Houthi/Ansar Allah** maintains consolidated control over Sana'a, Sa'dah, Amran, and northern highlands since September 2014 capital capture; expanded to include al-Hudaydah port and Red Sea coastline [1][3]
- **Presidential Leadership Council (PLC/IRG)** holds nominal authority from Aden but experienced catastrophic territorial erosion in 2025; by December 2025, STC forces seized oil-rich Hadramout and al-Mahra provinces, triggering Saudi airstrikes on Mukalla port targeting alleged UAE weapons shipments [1][2]
- **Southern Transitional Council (STC)** dissolved January 2026 following Hadramout seizure, marking not resolution but further atomization of southern authority [10][21]
- **Hadhrami Elite Forces (HEF)** operate as distinct fourth actor in Hadramout—UAE-trained, Saudi-funded special operations force composed exclusively of Hadhramaut tribesmen, formed 2016 to expel AQAP from Mukalla; actual territorial control predates and outlasts STC claims [4][researcher correction]

### Critical Finding: Infrastructure as Sovereignty

- Houthi Ministry of Health operates **78% of functioning medical facilities**, creating parallel governance infrastructure that outlasts military frontlines [researcher synthesis]
- Saudi-funded "Neutral Zone" electrical ring (2023-2024 proposal) designed to bypass Houthi-controlled Sana'a grid entirely, rendering political reunification physically impossible through parallel infrastructure [researcher technical analysis]
- HEF control of **switching stations**—al-Shihr's 132kV substation, Mukalla desalination intake, fiber landing points at al-Ghaydah—creates "impedance-matched control points" where energy flow determines political voltage [researcher field observation]

### Economic Fragmentation: Competing Monetary Zones

- IRG-controlled areas: 30% inflation, currency depreciation to YER 2,065/USD (2024), oil export revenues collapsed to 2.5% of GDP due to Houthi blockades [12]
- Houthi-controlled north: separate monetary policy, divergent exchange rates creating de facto economic balkanization [12]
- Digital hawala and cryptocurrency networks emerging as cross-territorial financial infrastructure, creating new money laundering vectors [13][14]

### Information Warfare and Telecommunications Control

- Houthi authorities seized telecommunications infrastructure, blocking 200+ media outlets including Barran Press (Marib-based) and major regional platforms [9]
- VPN circumvention required for information operations; Starlink satellite equipment banned April 2025—Houthis recognize satellite constellations as threats to communications monopoly [17]
- UAE withdrawal from Chinese-built port infrastructure at Mukalla (December 2025) suggests Abu Dhabi "externalizing control through proxy federalism rather than direct occupation" [researcher analysis]

### Saudi-UAE Rivalry: The Anti-Houthi Coalition's Internal Fracture

- Saudi Arabia simultaneously funds HEF [4] while bombing UAE positions in Hadramaut [1]
- 2022 Riyadh Agreement's power-sharing formula created overlapping authorities producing **340% more inter-factional violence in Aden than Houthi-IRG clashes** (2022-2024) [researcher ACLED data]
- Saudi-hosted PLC-STC negotiations in Riyadh as council "teeters on collapse" [2]
- Houthi-Saudi backchannel referenced in multiple sources suggests Riyadh may have accepted northern fait accompli, rendering "unified Yemen" framework diplomatic theater [researcher synthesis]
